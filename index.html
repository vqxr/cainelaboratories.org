The Standard of Care

Beyond Blind
Resection

Endometriosis affects ~10% of people with a uterus. The standard of care is surgical resection—but recurrence rates exceed 50% within five years. The problem is not surgical skill. Surgeons simply cannot reliably distinguish microscopic pathological tissue from healthy stroma by visual inspection.

Instead of targeting anatomy, we target the cellular program. We use Boolean logic circuits engineered from RNA to directly evaluate a cell's state. The circuit reads single-cell RNA-seq (scRNA-seq) expression levels:

Kill = (A > θ₁) ∧ (B < θ₂)

If the inputs match the high/low profile of the ectopic lesion, the gate triggers apoptosis. Otherwise, the cell is left unharmed.

Technical Pipeline

We Did Not Enumerate.
We Optimised.

Designing a circuit to kill ectopic lesions while sparing healthy tissue requires navigating millions of combinatorial marker states. We do not brute-force this.

We run a Constrained Optimisation over denoised scVI-VAE posterior estimates. The differentiable search finds the exact logic gate inputs that maximize Killing Accuracy (F1-score) while heavily penalizing off-target toxicity against a whole-body safety atlas like Tabula Sapiens.

The AI finds the logic. The atlas validates it.

Circuit Dynamics

Kinetics &
Clinical Validation.

A logic gate is only useful if it acts like a switch. The rate of change of targeted metabolism in our RNA circuits follows Hill function kinetics, governing how "snappy" the cell death trigger is.

v = V_max[S]ⁿ / (K_mⁿ + [S]ⁿ)

By tuning the Hill coefficient (n), we achieve extreme ultrasensitivity. The circuit does not "partially kill" a cell—it evaluates the boolean state, crosses the threshold, and executes.
