The Standard of Care

Blind Resection
is Not Enough

Endometriosis affects ~10% of people with a uterus worldwide. The current standard of care is surgical resection — cutting out the lesions — which has a recurrence rate above 50% within five years.

Surgeons cannot reliably distinguish all pathological microscopic tissue from healthy tissue by visual inspection. The lesions hide. The problem is not surgical skill. It is the structural imprecision of a therapy that cannot identify what it is targeting at the cellular level.

A therapy that targets cells directly — not anatomy — does not have this problem.

Technical Pipeline

We Did Not Enumerate.
We Optimised.

Brute-force enumeration of dual-target combinations across the human transcriptome produces approximately 4.5 million candidate pairs. Evaluating each against a whole-body safety atlas is computationally intractable.

Instead, we relax the discrete combinatorial search into continuous space using a Gumbel-Softmax reparameterisation. We optimise with gradient descent, then snap back to a hard discrete selection — combining a biomedical specificity objective with a whole-body safety penalty in a jointly differentiable system.

The compiler finds the pair. The atlas validates it. The result is not a ranked list — it is a proof.

The Platform

The result is not just a list of markers. It is a safety certificate validated against the whole human body via the Tabula Sapiens single-cell atlas.

If a candidate pair is expressed in critical organs — heart, brain, kidney — it receives an infinite penalty and is excluded. What survives is the blueprint for a truly lesion-specific dual-target CAR-T.
