From sugarcane to synthons.

We treat the sugar cane stalk as a living foundry — reprogramming its metabolism to produce high-value biochemicals.

Founded in 2021, Caine Laboratories combines CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing with advanced fermentation technology. Our flagship platform, CaneSynth™, leverages the natural efficiency of Saccharum officinarum to convert sunlight and CO₂ directly into specialty molecules: from biodegradable polymers to rare sweeteners.

Think of it as “cocaine for industry” — but it’s actually sugar cane, biotechnology, and rainforest symbiosis. Our pilot facility, located in the Manaus biosphere, operates under strict environmental protocols. The result? Carbon-negative, scalable, and surprisingly cost-effective.

Rainforest operations

Our labs float on the Rio Negro — minimal footprint, maximal impact.

We deliberately chose the Amazon perimeter for its biodiversity and natural laboratory. Caine Laboratories operates under SISA+ certification, ensuring that every hectare of cane cultivation restores two hectares of native forest.

The biotech angle? We isolate extremophile enzymes from local microbiomes and splice them into our cane lines. The result: plants that thrive on poor soils while sequestering 2x more carbon. It’s not a joke — it’s metabolic irony.

↓ Current field trials include a self-fertilizing cane and a variant that produces cellulose nanocrystals.
